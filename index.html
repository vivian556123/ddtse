DDTSE: Discriminative Diffusion Model
for Target Speech Extraction

Leying Zhang1,2, Yao Qian2, Linfeng Yu1,
Long Zhou2, Shujie Liu2, Yanmin Qian1, Michael Zeng2

1Shanghai Jiao Tong University, China   2Microsoft, USA


Abstract

While diffusion models have been demonstrated to be capable of generating higher-quality speech compared to discriminative models in certain speech-related tasks, the research on target speech extraction under noisy conditions remains relatively unexplored. Moreover, the superior quality typically comes at the cost of slower inference speed due to a larger number of denoising steps, limiting its practicality in real-world scenarios. In this paper, we introduce a discriminative diffusion model for target speech extraction. The training objectives of this model are designed to recover clean observations from their noisy counterparts. Furthermore, we devise a strategy to emulate the inference process during the model training. This model can also be directly utilized to enhance the performance of traditional discriminative models, without the need for additional retraining. We evaluated our proposed model on the Libri2Mix dataset under both noisy and clean conditions. Experimental results demonstrate that our model not only achieves higher perceptual quality compared to the discriminative model in noisy scenarios, but also accelerates the inference process by 3 times compared to the conventional diffusion model.
